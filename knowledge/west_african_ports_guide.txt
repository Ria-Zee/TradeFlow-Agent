WEST AFRICAN PORT INTELLIGENCE GUIDE
For African Import/Export Trade Decisions

APAPA PORT (Lagos, Nigeria)
Official Name: Apapa Container Terminal
Operator: Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) + Terminal Operators
Location: Lagos, Nigeria (coordinates: 6.4496° N, 3.3621° E)

Capacity: Approximately 1.2 million TEUs annually
Status: Operational - FREQUENTLY CONGESTED
Average Clearance Time: 7-21 days (highly variable)
Known Issues:
- Chronic truck congestion on Apapa-Wharf Road
- Terminal gridlock during peak periods (Q4 especially)
- Demurrage accumulation risk - containers not collected within free days accrue daily charges
- Power outages affecting scanning equipment
- Berth availability delays for vessel discharge

Demurrage Free Days: Typically 5-7 days (verify with shipping line)
Demurrage Rate: $35-75 per container per day after free period
Port Charges: Approximately $150-300 per TEU (terminal handling)
Documentation Required: Bill of Lading, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Form M, SONCAP certificate, Combined Certificate of Value and Origin

Best For: High-volume shipments, established importers with clearing agents

TIN CAN ISLAND PORT (Lagos, Nigeria)
Official Name: Tin Can Island Container Terminal
Operator: Nigerian Ports Authority + Grimaldi/ENL Consortium
Location: Lagos, Nigeria (adjacent to Apapa)

Capacity: Approximately 500,000 TEUs annually
Status: Operational - LESS CONGESTED than Apapa
Average Clearance Time: 5-14 days
Known Issues:
- Smaller capacity than Apapa
- Fewer direct shipping line calls
- Access road also subject to congestion

Port Charges: Approximately $120-250 per TEU
Best For: Mid-size shipments, importers seeking Apapa alternative
Advantage: Generally faster clearance, less truck congestion

COTONOU PORT (Benin Republic)
Official Name: Port Autonome de Cotonou
Operator: Government of Benin / SOBEMAP
Location: Cotonou, Benin Republic

Status: Operational - EFFICIENT
Average Clearance Time: 3-7 days (significantly faster than Nigerian ports)
Transit to Lagos: 3-5 hours by road (approximately 130km)

Advantages:
- Significantly faster clearance
- Less bureaucratic congestion
- Lower port charges than Nigerian ports
- Electronic manifest system well-implemented
- Preferred by experienced Nigerian traders for time-sensitive cargo

Disadvantages:
- Customs duty still payable in Nigeria upon entry
- Road transit risks (vehicle checkpoints)
- Additional transport cost ($200-500 per container Lagos delivery)
- Some re-export restrictions apply

Best For: Time-sensitive cargo, importers willing to manage road transit
Cost Comparison: Total landed cost often LOWER than Apapa despite transport due to faster clearance and lower demurrage

TEMA PORT (Accra, Ghana)
Official Name: Tema Port
Operator: Ghana Ports and Harbours Authority
Location: Tema, Ghana (30km east of Accra)

Status: Operational - EFFICIENT
Average Clearance Time: 3-5 days
Transit to Lagos: Approximately 500km by road

Best For: Regional distribution to West Africa (Ghana, Ivory Coast, Burkina Faso)
Note: Long transit distance to Nigeria makes this less practical for Nigeria-bound cargo unless cargo is for regional distribution

PORT SELECTION DECISION MATRIX:
Time Priority → Cotonou (fastest) or Tin Can Island
Cost Priority → Compare Apapa vs Cotonou total landed cost
Volume → Apapa for large volumes (more shipping line options)
Risk Mitigation → Cotonou or Tin Can Island (avoid Apapa congestion)
