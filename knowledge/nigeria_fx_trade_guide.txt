FOREIGN EXCHANGE AND PAYMENT GUIDE FOR NIGERIAN IMPORTERS

EXCHANGE RATE CONTEXT (as of mid-2026 estimates):
- USD/NGN Official Rate (CBN): approximately 1,300-1,400 NGN per USD
- Note: Exchange rates fluctuate daily - always verify current rates
- Historical volatility: Naira has experienced significant devaluation since 2023 CBN unification policy

FORM M REQUIREMENT:
All imports into Nigeria above $1,000 USD value require a Form M from the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) through a commercial bank.

What is Form M?
Form M is a mandatory import declaration document issued by Nigerian commercial banks on behalf of the CBN. It must be obtained BEFORE goods are shipped.

Process:
1. Open import documentation with your Nigerian bank
2. Bank submits Form M application to CBN Trade Monitoring System (TRMS)
3. CBN issues Form M number (typically 3-7 banking days)
4. Form M number must appear on all shipping documents
5. Bank opens Letter of Credit or accepts supplier invoice

Cost: Bank charges approximately 0.5-1% of invoice value for LC opening
Processing Time: 5-10 banking days total

PAYMENT METHODS FOR CHINESE SUPPLIERS:
Telegraphic Transfer (TT/Wire Transfer)
- Most common for established relationships
- Full payment or 30% deposit + 70% on BL copy
- Risk: Highest risk for new suppliers (payment before receipt)

Letter of Credit (LC)
- Most secure for buyer
- Bank guarantees payment only upon compliant documents
- Higher cost (bank charges both sides)
- Recommended for: New suppliers, large orders above $50,000

Trade Assurance (Alibaba)
- Available for verified Alibaba suppliers
- Escrow-style protection
- Good for first-time Chinese supplier relationships
- Limit: $1 million per transaction

FX RISK MANAGEMENT:
Given Naira volatility, importers should:
1. Calculate worst-case landed cost assuming 10% further Naira depreciation
2. Price goods to market at time of customs clearance, not at time of order
3. Consider forward contracts for large orders (available through GTBank, Zenith, UBA)
4. Minimize time between FX purchase and supplier payment

BANKS ACTIVE IN TRADE FINANCE (Nigeria):
- Zenith Bank (strong trade finance desk)
- GTBank (Guaranty Trust - excellent LC processing)
- UBA (United Bank for Africa - pan-African coverage)
- Access Bank (strong import documentation team)
- First Bank (largest branch network)
