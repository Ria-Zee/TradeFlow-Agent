SONCAP CERTIFICATION GUIDE FOR NIGERIAN IMPORTERS
Standards Organisation of Nigeria Conformity Assessment Programme

WHAT IS SONCAP?
SONCAP is a mandatory pre-shipment conformity assessment programme administered by the Standards Organisation of Nigeria (SON). It applies to regulated products being imported into Nigeria.

WHY IS IT MANDATORY?
Without a valid SONCAP Certificate, goods will be detained at Nigerian ports and cannot be cleared through customs. The Nigeria Customs Service (NCS) checks for SONCAP certificates as part of the import documentation review.

WHICH PRODUCTS REQUIRE SONCAP?
Regulated Products include but are not limited to:
- Mobile phones and smartphones (ALL brands)
- Laptops and computers
- Televisions and monitors
- Electrical cables and wires
- Solar panels and inverters
- Batteries (including lithium-ion)
- Electrical switches and sockets
- Tyres (automotive)
- Bottled water (requires NAFDAC, not SONCAP)
- Construction materials (cement, iron rods)
- Generating sets

HOW TO OBTAIN SONCAP CERTIFICATE:
Step 1: Register with an accredited SONCAP certification body
Accredited Bodies include: Intertek, Bureau Veritas, SGS, COTECNA, Alex Stewart

Step 2: Product Testing
- Laboratory testing against Nigerian Industrial Standards (NIS)
- Testing occurs at origin (in China, before shipment)
- Cost: $500-2000+ depending on product complexity
- Time: 2-6 weeks

Step 3: Factory Assessment (for new suppliers)
- Auditor visits factory
- Cost: $1500-3000
- Time: 1-3 weeks additional

Step 4: Certificate Issuance
- Valid for one shipment or one year (product family)
- Must be presented at port of entry

IMPORTANT TIMING CONSIDERATION:
SONCAP certification must be obtained BEFORE goods are shipped. It cannot be done after arrival in Nigeria. Plan for 4-8 weeks lead time minimum.

COST ESTIMATES (indicative):
Smartphones (new supplier): $2,000-4,000 total
Smartphones (approved supplier): $500-1,500 per shipment
Solar panels (new): $3,000-5,000 total
Solar panels (approved): $800-2,000 per shipment
